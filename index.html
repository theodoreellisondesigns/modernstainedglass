A working chart · figures & connections

The Modern Window, 1900–2010

Central figures of modern stained glass, arranged by national current (rows) and decade (columns). Europe builds a sustained modern tradition across the mid-century; America's contribution is early (Sullivan and Wright), then sparse, then reimported. Each card gives the figure's significance, an example work, and connections — cross-current links are colored to the current they reach — with a cell on the right to drop a representative image into.

Koloman Moser
1868–1918 · Vienna
Secession & Wiener Werkstätte; among the first geometric modern windows.
WorkKirche am Steinhof windows, Vienna (1907)
ConnectionsCo-founded Wiener Werkstätte (Hoffmann); grid vocabulary → Art Deco & the Bauhaus

Louis Sullivan
1856–1924 · Chicago
Father of modern American architecture and its organic ornament; made art glass integral to the building. Without him, no Wright.

The reversal

Sullivan → Wright → Wasmuth Portfolio (1910) → De Stijl & the Bauhaus. America's one early gift to the modern window — geometric abstraction — crossing east.

The bridge

Thorn Prikker → Albers → Jane Slater Marquis. The German root reaches American glass through one teacher and one student at Black Mountain — and ends in an outlier (◇): Marquis's descent is impeccable, her reach almost nil. The keystone example, not an influence.

The transmission

Schaffrath & Reyntiens teaching + Sowers's books → the 1970s American revival. The developed tradition, finally reimported.
